🌐🧱 Passive Pressurization via Self-Gravity of Walls

Kardashev range: 1.3–2.4

If habitat walls become massive enough, their own gravity starts helping hold onto atmosphere. Material strength is no longer required, whatsoever, to contain the inner pressure.

Notes

This points toward giant enclosed environments where atmospheric support comes partly from the structure itself, including the broader family of gravitational balloon concepts. The challenge of building it is obviously movement of mass into the locations where it is needed, but asteroid macroporosity could give us a good head start on this.

We just do not have much in the way of specific geometry for those voids right now, so we cannot say for sure until we explore them.

While material strength is not needed, there is still the problem of maintaining a pressure boundary via a liner. Essentially, some kind of sheet can rest on the rock wall while holding air on a molecular level.
